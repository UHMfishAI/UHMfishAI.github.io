about

Aloha! Our names are Brandon Nelson and Ronan Andal, and for our capstone project, we had the pleasure of collaborating with the innovative team at Ai.Fish LLC to help improve their Edge computing solutions for AI applications at sea.

About AI Fish

Ai.Fish is a cutting-edge computer vision and machine learning company with a strong focus on supporting sustainability-related projects. They specialize in delivering advanced image and video analytics at scale across various application areas, including fisheries management, ecological science, and conservation. Since our founding in 2019, Ai.Fish has been entirely grant and contract funded, solidifying our reputation as a leading artificial intelligence company in the marine conservation space. Building on this foundation, they are now expanding their product offerings, growing their customer and client base, and venturing into broader AI applications and markets.

The Problem

Ai.Fish is in the process of developing an Edge computing system designed to enable real-time computer vision analysis during fishing trips. This system serves as an alternative to traditional shore-based analysis of electronic monitoring footage after a fishing expedition. However, deploying Edge computing systems onboard fishing vessels presents unique challenges, including harsh environmental conditions, limited power availability, frequent power disruptions, and the need for continuous operation to process up to three weeks of 24/7 footage.

The Project

Our capstone project focuses on enhancing the prototype Edge computing system that recently underwent field testing. We tackle various product roadmap items aimed at extending and improving the system's functionality. Throughout the project, we gain invaluable experience in addressing low-power computing challenges, constructing and optimizing Linux operating system functionalities, and experimenting with hosting and launching web applications from low-powered devices.
